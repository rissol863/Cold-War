How did the Allies liberate Europe?

Sicily

The Sicilian Operation, or Operation Husky, was a World War II operation during which Allied forces defeated the Axis forces located in Sicily, and captured the island. The large-scale landing was followed by six weeks of fighting on land. The Sicilian operation marked the beginning of the Allied Italian Campaign.

More

Italy

Stuck on the formidable Gustav Line north of Naples in late 1943, the Allies outflanked flanked it, landing at Anzio and Nettuno, and then broke through it with a massive offensive in May 1944. By the fall of 1944, after more than a year of intense military operations, Rome and most of Italy were liberated.

Normandy

The Normandy Operation, or Operation Overlord, was a strategic operation of the Allies. on the landing of troops in Normandy, which began early in the morning of June 6, 1944 and ended on the 25th August 1944, after which the Allies crossed the Seine River, liberated Paris and continued their advance towards the French-German border.

Germany

In May 1945, after fierce fighting, Soviet troops took Berlin, the capital. This was the decisive event of World War II in Europe, as Berlin was the last major stronghold of the Nazi regime. Cold War intelligence operations

M.I.6 - Gold

  • In May 1968, a group of Soviet GRU special forces of up to 10 people carried out an attack on a secret American facility located on the territory of the Republic of Cambodia, 30 kilometers from the border with Vietnam. The Americans used this base to transport Vietnam of its reconnaissance groups, as well as forays to search for its special forces and downed pilots. In the parking lot of the complex, there were always 2 light helicopter, up to 10 transport helicopters, as well as 4 Cobra helicopters. The target of the attack was precisely these helicopters that at that time had a unique target guidance system and were controlled rockets. After 25 minutes of attack, one Cobra helicopter was hijacked to Vietnam, and the rest were destroyed. Losses among American soldiers amounted to 20 Human. The CIA only learned that the operation was carried out by special forces of the Soviet Union. several years later, as a result of an internal leak from the KGB.

CIA - Cyclone

  • Operation Cyclone was a secret CIA program aimed at supporting the Afghan Mujahideen in their fight against Soviet troops in Afghanistan in 1979-1989. Here are some details about the operation itself: Operation Plan Operation Cyclone was developed in 1980 by a team of CIA experts who worked closely with the Pakistani intelligence service (ISI). The plan involved supplying the mujahideen with weapons, ammunition, and other assistance, as well as training them in military matters. Weapons Supply The CIA supplied the mujahideen with weapons and ammunition through the Pakistani border. The main suppliers of weapons were the United States, Pakistan, and Saudi Arabia. The weapons included: Machine guns Sniper rifles Rocket launchers Mortars Artillery guns Mujahideen Training The CIA also trained the mujahideen in military matters, including tactics, strategy, and the use of weapons. The training was conducted at secret camps in Pakistan and Afghanistan. Funding Operation Cyclone was funded by the CIA and the Pakistani intelligence service (ISI). Funding was also provided by Saudi Arabia and other countries. The Role of Pakistani Intelligence Pakistani Intelligence (ISI) played a key role in Operation Cyclone. ISI provided the CIA with information on the Soviet troops and the mujahideen, and helped in supplying weapons and training the mujahideen. The Role of Saudi Intelligence Saudi Intelligence also played an important role in Operation Cyclone. Saudi Intelligence provided the CIA with information on the Soviet troops and the mujahideen, and helped in funding the operation. Results of the Operation Operation Cyclone was successful in achieving its goals. Soviet troops were forced to leave Afghanistan in 1989, and the mujahideen were able to establish control over the country. However, the operation also had negative consequences. The mujahideen supported by the CIA were often linked to radical Islamist groups that later became terrorist organizations such as al-Qaeda.
